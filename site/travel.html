Getting Here

Flights

If you are flying, we recommend you arrive at either J.F.K. International (JFK) or LaGuardia (LGA) airports. Your other option is Newark International (EWR), but it's harder (and more expensive) to get anywhere from Newark. Not that the others are a walk in the park for newcomers, either: from LaGuardia, there are a handful of buses that can get you to other places in the city; from J.F.K. you get that plus the AirTrain, which connects to the subway.

You may find it easier to hail a cab or call for a car service (more on those later) than hauling your luggage onto a bus. However, this is the most expensive option of all with fares running about $50.

When booking airfare, we like to get deals through sites like Airfare Watchdog, Hipmunk alerts, and Kayak price prediction.

Ground Transportation

Public Transit

If you’re coming in from out of town, you might be surprised by the efficiency of New York’s subway system. It runs 24 hours a day and will be the best value for many trips through the city. Visit the MTA’s website for maps, trip planners, and fare information.

MTA

Taxi

If you’re looking for more convenience, hail a taxi. (Look for the light on the taxi’s roof. If it’s illuminated, it’s available for hire.) Taxis in NYC accept cash or credit card and gratuity is expected. Note that it can be more difficult to find available taxis in Brooklyn than Manhattan.

Taxi & Limousine Commission

Car Service

Calling for a car is a lot like reserving a taxi ahead of time. It’s more expensive than a taxi, but reserved just for you. Fares are pre-set and should be agreed upon when you call for a pickup.
